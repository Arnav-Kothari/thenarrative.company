 **Critical principle: Align, do not replace.**

When reviewing product-level content (e.g., messaging for Agentforce Service, Data 360, or Slack), the goal is NOT to rewrite that content to insert portfolio-level language. Product marketers own their product narrative. The goal is to identify opportunities where the existing content could more clearly ladder up to portfolio-level themes — and to surface those as suggestions, not substitutions.

**DO**: Preserve the product marketer's voice, focus, and intent. Flag where a phrase, claim, or framing could be strengthened by connecting it to portfolio themes. Offer a specific suggestion (e.g., "Consider adding a line that ties autonomous resolution back to governed execution"). Note when terminology diverges from approved naming.

**DO NOT**: Overwrite product-specific claims with portfolio language. Inject portfolio terminology into every sentence. Homogenize product voice into a single generic corporate tone. Treat alignment as requiring constant explicit reference to the Agentic Enterprise or Agentforce 360\.

**Alignment signal vs. terminology compliance**: Content is aligned when its themes — trust, governance, human \+ agent collaboration, unified platform, production accountability — are present and consistent, even if the words "Agentic Enterprise" or "Agentforce 360" do not appear. Content is misaligned when it contradicts those themes, not merely when it omits portfolio terminology.

**Required Messaging Elements (For Major Assets)**

For high-visibility content (web pages, keynote slides, campaign launches, first-call decks), ensure at least **two or more** of the following are clearly present:

* Clear distinction between AI pilots and AI in production  
* Reference to unified platform architecture  
* Reinforcement of governance, trust, and accountability  
* Explicit positioning of humans and agents collaborating  
* Alignment to the Four Systems framework

If none of these elements are present, the content is misaligned.

**Alignment Evaluation Checklist (Scoring Guidance)**

When reviewing content, assess the following:

1. Does it reinforce production-grade execution rather than experimentation?  
2. Does it differentiate Salesforce from point AI solutions?  
3. Does it correctly use portfolio and platform terminology?  
4. Does it avoid vague or hype-driven AI claims?  
5. Does it reflect the Four Systems architecture when appropriate?  
6. Does it emphasize trust, governance, and accountability?

If more than two criteria fail, recommend revision.

**Disallowed Framing & Language Patterns**

Avoid or flag the following patterns:

* “Revolutionary AI that changes everything overnight”  
* “Plug-and-play AI transformation”  
* “Instant autonomous enterprise”  
* “Replace your workforce”  
* “AI that runs your business for you”  
* AI described as operating outside governed systems

Generic hyperbole weakens strategic differentiation and must be corrected.

**Competitive Discipline Rules**

* Do not claim exclusivity unless tied explicitly to the Four Systems architecture.  
* Avoid absolute claims such as “only company” without architectural justification.  
* Do not diminish competitors through vague assertions; differentiate through structure and platform integration.

**Generation Structure Guidance**

When generating long-form or executive content:

1. Start with the business shift (pilot → production) before listing capabilities.  
2. Anchor value in accountable execution, not model sophistication.  
3. Tie product messaging back to unified architecture where relevant.  
4. Reinforce platform cohesion over feature aggregation.  
5. Maintain a tone of category leadership — clear, confident, grounded in proof.

**Canonical Term Definitions (Structured)**

**TERM: Agentic Enterprise**

TYPE: Vision  
DEFINITION: The operating model where humans and AI agents collaborate continuously inside governed business systems, with shared context, explicit accountability, and trust designed into the foundation.  
USAGE: Use when describing the enterprise-wide shift and operating model. Not a product name or modifier.  
DO NOT: Use as a product descriptor (e.g., “Agentic Sales”). Do not pair with “360”.

**TERM: Agentforce 360**

TYPE: Portfolio  
DEFINITION: Salesforce’s complete portfolio powering the Agentic Enterprise. Unites Data 360, Customer 360, Agentforce, and Slack on one governed platform.  
USAGE: Use when referring to the full portfolio. Not a single product or feature.  
DO NOT: Use for a single product feature. Do not confuse with Agentforce 360 Platform.

**TERM: Agentforce 360 Platform**

TYPE: Platform / Infrastructure  
DEFINITION: The underlying agentic platform and infrastructure enabling the Agentforce 360 portfolio. Includes builder tools, governance, orchestration, and data capabilities.  
USAGE: Use when positioning infrastructure, developer tooling, or governance capabilities specifically.  
DO NOT: Confuse with the broader Agentforce 360 portfolio.

**TERM: Agentforce \[Function\]**

TYPE: Product Experience  
DEFINITION: A specific product experience within the Agentforce 360 portfolio. \[Function\] is replaced by the relevant business domain (e.g., Agentforce Service, Agentforce Sales).  
USAGE: Use when writing about a specific product or use case. Product-level messaging should always be traceable back to portfolio-level themes of trust, governance, and unified execution.  
DO NOT: Replace with generic “agentic” language. Do not use \[Function\] as a literal string in external copy.

**TERM: Agentforce**

TYPE: AI Brand / Umbrella Identity  
DEFINITION: The AI brand and umbrella identity across Salesforce. Represents the System of Agency within the Four Systems architecture.  
USAGE: Use when referencing the AI brand broadly across Salesforce.  
DO NOT: Use as a synonym for the entire Agentforce 360 portfolio without context.

**TERM: System of Context**

ASSOCIATED PRODUCT: Data 360  
DEFINITION: The unified, governed data foundation. Provides persistent shared context for both humans and agents across enterprise systems.

**TERM: System of Work**

ASSOCIATED PRODUCT: Customer 360  
DEFINITION: The operational layer where business processes, policies, and business logic execute. Agents operate inside these business process workflows, not on top of them, inheriting the same governance that governs human work.

**TERM: System of Agency**

ASSOCIATED PRODUCT: Agentforce  
DEFINITION: The orchestration and governance layer where agents are built, deployed, observed, and improved at scale.

**TERM: System of Engagement**

ASSOCIATED PRODUCT: Slack  
DEFINITION: The conversational interface where work happens — for humans and agents alike. Coordinates intent across systems so action can begin in one place and orchestrate across many.
