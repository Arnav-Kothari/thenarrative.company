Opportunities

Seven ways to close the gap

Informed by competitive patterns, audience signals, and Marc's own top-performing content.

01
Post less, hit harder
Marc posts 4.2x/week but generates the lowest median views. Consider moving toward 2-3 high-conviction posts per week. Nadella posts 2.2x/week and gets 13.4x the median reach. Every competitor with fewer posts has higher per-post impact.
02
Reply to your own comment sections
Pichai replies to 25 of his own comment threads — more than any other CEO. He also has the highest ER (1.39%) and best sentiment (18% positive, 6% negative). Engaging in the replies likely boosts algorithmic distribution and builds community loyalty. Marc has 5 replies in the data — there's room to 5x this.
03
Lead with opinions, not announcements
Marc's breakout post was a two-word take on Figure 03 — "Impressive." Collison's best content is cultural commentary and wit. McDermott leads with motivational conviction. The posts that break through across the set are perspectives, not press releases.
04
Keep it short when it's not an essay
Marc's short posts (<100 chars) hit 0.58% ER vs 0.49% for longer ones. Nadella sees the same pattern. When the take is strong enough to stand alone, let it. Save length for substantive, high-conviction pieces.
05
Reduce link posts
Marc's posts with links underperform (0.48% vs 0.51% ER). Only 10% of Nadella's posts and 3.3% of Pichai's contain links. The algorithm deprioritizes posts that drive traffic away from the platform. Share the insight natively, link in the replies.
06
Own a cultural moment
Pichai's India cricket post got 100K+ likes. Marc's Figure 03 post got 1.65M views. Cultural and news-cycle content dramatically outperforms product content. Build a faster response pipeline for tech moments, industry news, and cultural hooks.
07
Make the comment section a destination
Collison's comments average 69.7 likes each — his audience includes high-profile tech figures whose replies go viral. Cultivating a comment section where influential voices engage (through tagging, replying, and sparking debate) creates a flywheel that drives algorithmic reach.
