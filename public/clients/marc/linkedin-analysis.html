Insight 2

High-Reach vs. High-Engagement Posts Are Different

The posts that get the most impressions and the posts that get the best engagement rate are two different groups. Understanding this distinction is key to deciding what each post should optimize for.

Reach Posts

Announcement-style posts drive impressions

The #1 impression post (Oct 29, 2024 - 1.93M impressions) dwarfs everything else by 2x+. Top reach posts cluster around product launches, earnings moments, and industry hot-takes. These reach far beyond Marc's followers but generate lower engagement rates (often under 1%).

Engagement Posts

Personal, values-driven posts drive deeper interaction

The highest engagement rates (2.4-2.9%) come from posts that feel personal, opinionated, or culturally relevant. The Feb 25 earnings post hit 2.89% - the best rate of any major post - with 11,537 engagements and 400K impressions. The audience leans in when Marc shares a perspective.

Insight 3

Posting Consistency Matters More Than Any Single Post

The months with the strongest overall performance aren't always the ones with a single viral hit. They're the ones with consistent posting cadence that keeps the baseline high.

Monthly impressions, new followers, and engagement rate

Key finding: When impressions dropped below 700K in a month (Sep 2025: 487K, Jan 2026: 508K), follower growth dropped to its lowest levels (8K and 7.5K). But even months without a breakout post maintained strong growth when the cadence stayed high - October 2025 hit 1.19M impressions and 14.9K new followers through multiple solid-performing posts rather than one viral moment. The engagement rate has also trended upward - from 1.28% in March 2025 to a peak of 2.27% in Feb 2026.

Going Forward

Recommendations

Based on 18 months of data, here's what the numbers tell us about what to do more of, optimize, and test.

  • Do More Lean into opinion and values-driven posts. The highest engagement rates (2.4-2.9%) come from posts where Marc takes a clear stance or shares something personal. These build the kind of audience loyalty that compounds over time.
  • Do More Experiment with longer-form narrative posts. The engagement rate has been climbing, suggesting the audience is ready for more depth. Testing 800-1200 word posts that tell a story - about Agentforce customers, AI trends, or leadership lessons - could push engagement above the current 2.3% ceiling.
  • Optimize Pair every big announcement with a personal follow-up. Launch/announcement posts drive massive reach but low engagement. A day-later follow-up with Marc's personal take on the same topic would capture that influx of new viewers and convert them to followers.
  • Optimize Cross-promote external appearances back to LinkedIn. The March 2026 spike (+5,722 followers in a day from an off-platform event) shows that media appearances and live events can drive LinkedIn growth when there's a clear path back to the profile. Every TV hit, podcast, or keynote should be followed by a LinkedIn post within 24 hours.
  • Test Higher posting frequency and its impact on quality metrics. Posting 2-3x per week correlates with stronger follower growth - months with more posts consistently added 13K-16K followers vs. 8K-10K in lighter months. But more posts may not improve average impressions or average engagement per post. Worth tracking whether a higher cadence dilutes per-post performance or whether the volume makes up for it.
