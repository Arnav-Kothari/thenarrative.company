Opportunities

Eight ways to close the gap

Informed by competitive patterns, audience signals, and Salesforce's own top-performing content.

01
Tighten the content mix
Consider shifting from 11/week toward 5-7 higher-impact posts. Anthropic posts 3.6x/week and drives 115 comments per post. The bandwidth freed up can go toward deeper creative on each post.
02
Evolve the voice
Salesforce's humor content already hits 0.90% ER — proof the team knows how to do this. The opportunity is making that conversational tone the default. Lead with insight, not product specs.
03
Broaden the content themes
Customer results with specifics ("OpenTable cut resolution time by 40%"), cultural hooks (F1 AI Fan Companion hit 107K views), and CEO amplification (79K median views) all complement Agentforce content well.
04
Use retweeting as a curation lever
The best-performing competitor handles use retweeting strategically — surfacing high-quality content from partners, employees, and the ecosystem. This turns the brand account into a destination people check in on regularly, not just a broadcast channel.
05
Invest in comment engagement
Google runs a 10.0% positive / 1.8% negative ratio with active community engagement (260 brand replies). HubSpot is at 19.2% / 2.0%. Proactive, personality-driven engagement in the replies can shift sentiment.
06
Try the short + media formula
Posts under 100 characters with attached media drive the highest ER across competitors. ServiceNow gets 1.34% ER with question-based posts — a format Salesforce uses in only 12.1% of content today.
07
Build on cultural moments
The MrBeast campaign showed what's possible. Google's Maps launch hit 28.6M views. Building a faster creative pipeline for cultural and news-driven content can create more breakthrough moments.
08
Amplify the Marc Benioff flywheel
Marc's posts hit 79K median views — higher than the brand account. Anthropic's brand is deeply connected to its leadership voice. Cross-posting and bridging CEO content with the brand account is a unique advantage.
