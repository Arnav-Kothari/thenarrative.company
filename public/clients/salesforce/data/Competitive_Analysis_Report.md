# Competitive X/Twitter Analysis: Salesforce vs. The Field

**Prepared for:** Salesforce Social Team
**Date:** March 2026
**Data window:** Dec 2025 — Mar 2026
**Accounts analyzed:** @salesforce (140 posts), @HubSpot (13 posts), @ServiceNow (17 posts), @Ryanair (44 posts)

---

## The Headline Numbers

| Account | Posts | Avg Views | Avg Likes | Avg Reposts | Avg Replies | Aggregate ER | Avg Comments/Post |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Salesforce** | 140 | 50,314 | 75 | 12 | 12 | **0.197%** | 5.0 |
| HubSpot | 13 | 3,695 | 16 | 3 | 5 | **0.645%** | 4.0 |
| ServiceNow | 17 | 1,914 | 13 | 3 | 3 | **0.947%** | 1.7 |
| Ryanair | 44 | 1,314,851 | 14,698 | 1,062 | 576 | **1.242%** | 107.1 |

**The core problem:** Salesforce has the biggest reach (50K avg views vs. competitors' 2-4K) but the lowest engagement rate in the set at 0.197%. That means people *see* the content but don't feel compelled to interact with it. HubSpot gets 3.3x the engagement rate. ServiceNow gets 4.8x. Ryanair gets 6.3x.

Salesforce is winning impressions but losing conversation.

---

## Part 1: What the Competition Does Well

### HubSpot — The Voice Winner

HubSpot posts roughly once per week (1.1/week) but every post feels like a person wrote it, not a brand. Their engagement rate (0.645%) is 3.3x Salesforce's despite being a fraction of the size.

**What they nail:**
- **Conversational, lowercase voice.** No corporate polish. Posts read like a group chat: "everything from now on is a January problem" / "OOO: officially unsupervised til 2026" / "just sign it"
- **Self-deprecating humor that lands.** "how to 5x your deal close rate: 1. open garbage can 2. place your legacy CRM inside 3. migrate to HubSpot instead" — this is product marketing disguised as a joke
- **Short, punchy copy.** Their best-performing posts are under 100 chars (0.85% ER vs 0.56% for medium-length)
- **Relatable moments over product specs.** They anchor content in the *feeling* of being a sales/marketing person, not in Breeze AI feature lists
- **Minimal links, minimal hashtags.** Only 7.7% of posts contain a link. They're building feed presence, not driving clicks
- **Positive comment section.** 19.2% of comments carry positive sentiment (vs. Salesforce's 6.8%). People *like* hanging out in their replies

**Their top post** (6,769 views, 0.71% ER): a fake pharmaceutical ad about "fixing your marketing funnel." Pure entertainment with brand embedded in it.

### ServiceNow — The Quiet Strategist

ServiceNow is small (17 posts, 1.5/week) but punches above its weight with a 0.947% ER — nearly 5x Salesforce's.

**What they nail:**
- **Hook-first copywriting.** "Enterprise AI promised less work. Instead, you got homework." / "Everyone bought into AI. Few are seeing the return." — these are provocative openers that stop the scroll
- **Cultural relevance.** Their F1 x Aston Martin content (1.37% ER) and music references (Backstreet Boys at Knowledge) tap into culture rather than just tech
- **Concise event marketing with personality.** Instead of "Register for Knowledge," they write: "This is one ball you shouldn't let drop."
- **Questions drive engagement.** 35.3% of their posts contain questions (vs. Salesforce's 12.1%), and those posts hit 1.34% ER — their highest format
- **Short posts win.** Posts under 100 chars average 1.64% ER

### Ryanair — The Engagement Benchmark

Ryanair is in a different category entirely (B2C, mass consumer), but they're included because they represent *what maximum engagement looks like* on X. 1.83% avg ER. 107 comments per post. Their playbook:

- **95.5% of posts are under 100 characters.** "Ryanhair." "me at passport control." "random seat allocation." The algorithm rewards brevity.
- **Media-first (72.7% have images/video)** but the copy does the work. The image is the punchline; the text is the setup.
- **Culturejacking at speed.** They jump on news (Elon Musk WiFi drama, Turkey-related trends) in hours, not days.
- **Brand as character, not corporation.** Ryanair *is* the snarky friend. Every post has a consistent personality. You follow them for entertainment, not information.
- **Zero links.** They almost never link out. The platform is the destination.

## Part 2: Where Salesforce Is Falling Short

### 1. The Agentforce Saturation Problem

37.1% of all Salesforce posts mention Agentforce. But here's the kicker:

| Content Type | % of Posts | Avg Views | Avg ER |
|---|---|---|---|
| Agentforce posts | 37.1% | 8,123 | 0.635% |
| Non-Agentforce posts | 62.9% | 75,245 | 0.480% |

Agentforce posts have *higher engagement rate* than non-Agentforce content — but dramatically lower reach. The non-Agentforce content gets 9x the views because it includes the MrBeast campaign, which was Salesforce's breakout moment (4.8M views on the top post). Strip that out, and the typical Salesforce post lives in the 2-6K view range with an ER below 1%.

The issue isn't that Agentforce content is bad. It's that it's repetitive and product-centric. The audience has been told Agentforce exists. Dozens of times. Over and over. The comment section reflects this — people aren't engaging because there's nothing new to react to.

### 2. The Voice Problem

Compare these:

**Salesforce:** "Most enterprise work needs precision and reasoning together. Enterprise AI isn't choosing between LLMs and deterministic..."

**HubSpot:** "everything from now on is a January problem"

**ServiceNow:** "Enterprise AI promised less work. Instead, you got homework."

Salesforce writes like a press release. HubSpot writes like a human. ServiceNow writes like a sharp marketer. The Salesforce feed reads as *institutional* — technically correct, product-forward, but emotionally flat.

### 3. Overposting, Underperforming

Salesforce posts 11.1x/week — 10x HubSpot's rate, 7x ServiceNow's. More posts doesn't mean more impact. It means more mediocre content diluting the feed. When you post 11 times a week and your median post gets 5,430 views and 18 likes, most of that content is effectively invisible.

### 4. The Comment Section Is a Red Flag

- 7.1% of comments on Salesforce posts are complaints/negative
- Only 6.8% are positive (compare to HubSpot's 19.2%)
- Real users are publicly complaining about Agentforce being deployed on help.salesforce.com and failing: *"agentforce is brainless and doesn't even understand its own help.salesforce platform"*
- The top repeat "commenter" is @salesforce itself (26 self-replies). The brand is talking to itself more than the audience is talking back.

### 5. Format Mismatches

- **Short posts (<=100 chars) get the fewest likes** on the Salesforce account (avg 0.26% ER), opposite of every competitor where short posts win. Why? Because Salesforce's "short" posts tend to be cryptic MrBeast puzzle teasers, not standalone punchy content.
- **Only 9.3% of posts use hashtags** — not necessarily bad, but hashtags deliver Salesforce's *best* ER (0.91%), suggesting there's untapped discoverability.
- **52.1% of posts use @mentions**, which should drive engagement, but ER on those (0.61%) is barely above average. The mentions aren't sparking real conversation.

## Part 3: What Salesforce Should Do About It

### Strategic Recommendations

**1. Cut the volume in half. Make every post earn its spot.**

Go from 11/week to 5-6/week. Every post should pass the test: "Would a human stop scrolling for this?" Right now, half the feed is product copy that reads like internal marketing briefs posted verbatim. Less content, better content.

**2. Develop a distinctive voice — and it shouldn't sound like a press release.**

HubSpot sounds like a witty coworker. ServiceNow sounds like a sharp analyst. Ryanair sounds like a provocateur. Salesforce sounds like... a corporation. The brand voice needs a personality injection.

Concrete shifts:
- Drop the title case. Drop the em dashes. Write in sentence case, conversational tone.
- Lead with the *insight*, not the product. "Enterprise AI promised less work. Instead, you got homework." (ServiceNow) > "Agentforce delivers autonomous AI agents that transform enterprise workflows." (Salesforce-style)
- Use the "reply-to-the-timeline" format. React to industry moments, not just your own product cycle.

**3. Diversify away from Agentforce as a topic.**

Agentforce in 37% of posts is too much. The audience gets it — Salesforce has AI agents. Now give them reasons to care that aren't product specs:
- **Customer results with specifics:** "OpenTable cut resolution time by 40%" is better than "Agentforce transforms customer service"
- **Cultural hooks:** The F1 AI Fan Companion (107K views, 0.50% ER) worked because it was contextual and timely
- **Benioff as a character:** Marc's posts get 79K avg views — the CEO voice outperforms the brand account on a per-post basis. Lean into that.

**4. Fix the comment section problem.**

- The Agentforce-on-help.salesforce complaint thread is a reputation risk sitting in public. Those comments need a real human response, not silence.
- Engage in replies as a personality, not a support bot. HubSpot's comment section feels warm because they interact. Salesforce's feels abandoned.
- Reduce self-reply threads (26 of them). Threads can work, but only if the content in each reply is independently valuable. Replying to yourself with "Register now:" is not engagement — it's a CTA dump.

**5. Lean into what already works.**

The data shows Salesforce's *own* best-performing themes:

| Theme | Avg ER | What Works |
|---|---|---|
| Humor/Meme | 0.90% | Highest ER — there's appetite for personality |
| Partnership | 0.80% | Co-branded content gets traction |
| Product/Feature | 0.78% | When tied to a specific, tangible use case |
| Culture/People | 0.73% | Trailblazer content performs |
| Thought Leadership | 0.70% | Only when it's provocative, not generic |

Humor and partnerships outperform AI/Agent content (0.65% ER). The brand is under-indexing on what its own audience responds to.

**6. Adopt the "short + media" formula.**

Across every competitor, posts under 100 characters with attached media drive the highest engagement rates. Ryanair averages 1.88% ER with this format. HubSpot hits 0.85%. For Salesforce, the data suggests:
- Pair a one-line hook with a strong image or video
- Save the longer copy for threads or blog links
- Ask more questions — Salesforce only asks questions in 12.1% of posts, while ServiceNow does it in 35.3% at 1.34% ER

**7. Build a rapid-response muscle.**

Ryanair's biggest posts are culturejacks — jumping on trending moments within hours. Salesforce's MrBeast campaign proved the brand *can* generate cultural conversation (4.8M views). But that was a planned activation. The opportunity is doing that reactively:
- Industry news drops (competitor stumbles, regulation changes, earnings surprises)
- Cultural moments (Super Bowl, F1 races, viral tech moments)
- Platform-native trends (meme formats, community jokes)

This requires giving the social team creative latitude to post without three rounds of legal review.

---

## The Bottom Line

Salesforce has the reach but not the resonance. The brand is posting at corporate-publication volume with corporate-publication energy, and the engagement numbers reflect it. The competitors are smaller but sharper — HubSpot with voice, ServiceNow with hooks, Ryanair with personality.

The fix isn't complicated: post less, say more, sound human, and give the audience something to react to beyond product announcements.

The MrBeast campaign proved the ceiling is high when Salesforce takes creative swings. The question is whether the daily content can carry even 10% of that energy.
